Literature Survey


Predicting mango variety using image processing is a relatively recent development in the field of agricultural technology. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have proven to be particularly effective for fruit classification due to their ability to capture intricate patterns and features in images. Studies such as the work by Shrivastava et al. (2020) have demonstrated the use of CNNs for identifying different mango varieties with high accuracy, showing that deep learning models can successfully differentiate between varieties based on visual characteristics.

Leaf disease identification using image processing is a well-established area of research, with CNNs being widely employed due to their superior performance in image recognition tasks. The techniques used have evolved significantly, moving from basic image preprocessing and feature extraction methods to more sophisticated deep learning models. For example, Sladojevic et al. (2016) have illustrated the effectiveness of CNNs in identifying multiple plant diseases with high precision, highlighting the potential for these models to be applied to various crops, including mangoes.

While significant progress has been made in the identification of leaf diseases, the automated recommendation of prevention strategies remains less explored. Most studies focus on the identification of diseases, creating a gap in the research related to actionable recommendations for disease management. Research by Picon et al. (2019) discusses the potential of integrating image processing with expert systems to recommend treatments, suggesting that further development is needed to bridge the gap between disease identification and prevention strategies. This integration could provide a comprehensive solution for managing plant health in mango orchards.

The use of image processing for predicting ripeness and grading of mangoes is gaining traction as a practical application of CNNs. These models can analyze visual cues such as color, texture, and shape to determine the ripeness of the fruit. Research by Sarker et al. (2019) has shown that CNN-based models can grade fruits with substantial accuracy, indicating their potential for practical use in sorting and grading processes. Such advancements can significantly aid in ensuring the quality and market value of mango produce by categorizing them into different grades based on their ripeness.

Mango Bloosom Application cultivation covers a broad spectrum, from variety prediction and disease identification to ripeness prediction and grading. However, there remains a notable research gap in the integration of these tasks into a single, cohesive system, and in the development of automated prevention strategy recommendations post disease identification. Addressing these gaps can lead to the development of comprehensive solutions that enhance the management and optimization of mango orchards. By leveraging the capabilities of CNNs and deep learning, it is possible to create a system that not only identifies issues but also provides actionable recommendations, ultimately benefiting mango producers through improved efficiency and productivity.


Research Gap


Research Gap for Domain 1: Integrated Systems

Current research largely focuses on individual tasks such as variety prediction, disease identification, and ripeness grading. Few studies have attempted to integrate these functionalities into a single comprehensive system. An integrated approach would streamline orchard management by providing a unified platform for identifying mango varieties, diagnosing diseases, and grading fruit ripeness, enhancing overall efficiency and usability for farmers.


Research Gap for Domain 2: Disease Prevention Recommendations

While disease identification using CNNs is well-researched, there is a notable gap in providing actionable prevention strategies post diagnosis. Existing studies primarily concentrate on the accurate detection of diseases, but they often stop short of suggesting practical measures to mitigate these issues. Developing systems that not only identify diseases but also recommend specific, evidence-based prevention strategies would be invaluable for effective orchard management and disease control.


Research Gap for Domain 3: Real-Time Application

Implementing these advanced image processing techniques in real-time scenarios presents significant challenges. Real-time application requires robust computational resources and efficient algorithms capable of operating under varying environmental conditions. The current state of research has yet to fully address these practical limitations, which include handling large datasets, ensuring fast processing times, and maintaining accuracy in diverse and dynamic orchard environments. Overcoming these challenges is crucial for creating practical, on-the-ground solutions that can be widely adopted by mango farmers.


Research Gap Overall Summary:

Addressing these research gaps involves developing integrated systems that combine variety prediction, disease identification, and ripeness grading, enhancing the functionality and practicality of these technologies. Additionally, incorporating disease prevention recommendations and optimizing algorithms for real-time application are essential steps toward making these advancements more accessible and beneficial for the agricultural sector.




Research Problem


Mango orchards face numerous challenges that impact productivity and quality, including the accurate identification of mango varieties, timely detection and management of leaf diseases, and effective grading of mango ripeness. Current technologies often address these issues in isolation, lacking a cohesive approach that integrates all aspects of orchard management into a single system. This fragmentation results in inefficiencies and missed opportunities for optimizing production and quality.

The Need for Integration

The identification of mango varieties is crucial for various agricultural and commercial purposes. Different varieties have unique growth requirements, market values, and disease susceptibilities. Accurate and timely identification can aid in better management practices and marketing strategies. While CNNs have shown high accuracy in fruit classification, their application remains limited to standalone tasks without integration into broader management systems.



Disease Identification and Management

Leaf diseases significantly affect mango yield and quality. Early detection and management are vital to prevent widespread damage. Although CNNs have proven effective in identifying plant diseases, the transition from identification to actionable prevention strategies is underdeveloped. Existing research often stops at detection, leaving a gap in the practical application of these findings to suggest and implement preventive measures. This gap needs addressing to provide a complete solution that encompasses both detection and management.



Ripeness Prediction and Grading

Ripeness prediction and grading are essential for ensuring mangoes meet market standards and consumer expectations. Proper grading based on ripeness can maximize market value and reduce post-harvest losses. CNNs can analyze visual cues such as color and texture to determine ripeness stages, but their application is often restricted to controlled environments and not integrated with other orchard management tasks.



Real-Time Application Challenges

Implementing these technologies in real-time scenarios presents additional challenges. Mango orchards are dynamic environments where lighting, weather, and other conditions vary, complicating the deployment of image processing systems. Real-time application requires robust computational resources and efficient algorithms capable of maintaining accuracy and speed under diverse conditions. Overcoming these practical limitations is crucial for the widespread adoption of these technologies in real-world settings.



Comprehensive Solution

The research problem, therefore, is to develop an integrated image processing system using CNNs that can simultaneously predict mango varieties, identify leaf diseases, recommend preventive strategies, and grade mango ripeness. Such a system would streamline orchard management by providing a unified platform for all these tasks, enhancing efficiency and productivity. The system should be capable of real-time operation, adapting to the dynamic conditions of mango orchards. Addressing these research gaps will result in a comprehensive tool that supports farmers in making informed decisions, ultimately leading to better management practices, improved yield, and higher-quality mangoes.



Objectives

Main Objective:

1. Identify Fresh Mangoes and Categorize Based on Usage: Develop a system to classify mangoes according to their optimal use, enhancing quality and market value. 2. Monitor and Control Spreadable Mango Diseases: Implement techniques to detect and manage diseases, preventing widespread outbreaks in orchards.

Sub Objective 1: Predict Mango Cultivation Variety:

Utilize Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to classify mango varieties based on fruit images. This will help in managing different varieties effectively and optimizing cultivation practices.

Sub Objective 2: Identify Mango Leaf Diseases

Develop a CNN model capable of detecting common mango leaf diseases with high accuracy. Early detection will enable timely interventions and reduce crop loss.

Sub Objective 3: Recommend Disease Prevention Strategies

Integrate an expert system with the disease identification model to provide actionable prevention measures. This will assist farmers in implementing effective disease management practices.

Sub Objective 4: Identify Mango Fruit Diseases

Create a CNN model specifically designed to detect diseases affecting mango fruits. This will ensure the health and quality of the harvested produce.

Sub Objective 5: Recommend Fruit Disease Prevention Strategies

Extend the expert system to offer preventive measures for fruit diseases, complementing the leaf disease management strategies and ensuring comprehensive protection.

Sub Objective 6: Predict Mango Ripeness and Grade

Implement a CNN-based model to assess the ripeness of mangoes. The model will classify the fruits into grades A, B, and C based on ripeness, aiding in quality control and market categorization.



These research sub-objectives collectively represent a holistic and transformative approach to Mango cultivation in Sri Lanka. By automating disease detection, improving agricultural practices, and fostering orchard maintainance, this study aspires to revitalize the region's Mango farming sector. The research endeavors to fortify food security, bolster the economic resilience of farmers, and usher in a more sustainable era of agriculture, thereby fostering long-term prosperity for both communities and the environment.

Methodology



Data Collection and Dataset Preparation

To enhance the accuracy of the model, a dataset consisting of nearly 1000 images was collected from mango orchards in Munthalama and Jaffna. Additionally, 2000 images were sourced from Kaggle. Subsequently, the dataset was filtered to remove unclear images. To facilitate disease identification, the dataset was organized into five folders, each corresponding to a specific disease. The images were then placed into the respective folders based on the type of disease they depicted. Field visits were conducted to gather data and information for research. One visit was made to Prof. Nishantha Giguruwa's mango orchard in Mundalama, while another visit was undertaken to a mango orchard in the Jaffna area. These visits provided valuable insights and data for the study. Furthermore, the collection of diseased fruit images and information on disease prevention strategies was carried out under the supervision of Prof. Nishantha Giguruwa and Suni Galearachchi, a Consultant Agronomist. Their expertise and guidance significantly contributed to the success of this research. These efforts collectively contributed to the development of a robust dataset for training and testing the CNN models, enabling accurate identification and classification of mango fruit diseases.

Data Augmentation

Image augmentation is a crucial technique used to expand datasets artificially, especially when working with limited data. This approach plays a significant role in enhancing the robustness and generalization capability of machine learning models. In our study, we employed the image augmenter provided by the Keras library to augment our dataset. By manipulating various augmentation parameters, we were able to generate additional images from a single image, effectively increasing the size of our dataset. Our augmentation process involved setting several parameters to create diverse variations of the original images.

Model Development

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), renowned for their efficacy in image classification tasks, were selected as the primary model for mango cultivar classification. These deep learning algorithms were adapted to accommodate the specific nuances and characteristics of mango cultivars, ensuring optimal classification outcomes. A suitable CNN architecture was selected based on considerations such as computational efficiency, accuracy, and classification performance. The CNN model underwent training using the augmented dataset, enabling accurate identification and differentiation of mango cultivars based on their unique attributes. By implementing this methodology, the research aims to develop a robust system capable of accurately classifying and distinguishing various mango cultivars, thereby facilitating informed decision-making in mango cultivation and management practices.
